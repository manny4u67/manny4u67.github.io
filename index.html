This dashboard summarizes the key operational issues identified in the warehouse. The chart below visualizes the primary categories of waste we need to address. Use this as a starting point to explore the specific problems and the proposed solutions based on Kaizen principles.

Detailed Problem Areas

Here we break down the core issues into three themes: Communication, Inventory, and Workflow. These are direct observations from the team, highlighting the real-world impact of our current processes. Click on each card to see specific examples.

Communication & Planning

Breakdowns in communication and a lack of forward planning are root causes of many downstream issues, leading to reactive work and stress.

Finding: "Miscommunication/Lack of communication... Information not relayed down the line."

Finding: "Planning can not be made on many situations because of lack of communication."

Finding: "Everything gets put in at 15 minutes or less, the press room does not plan ahead."

Space & Inventory

Our warehouse space is compromised by overstock and parts produced far in advance of need, creating clutter and inefficiency.

Example: "Abundance of Souriau parts... scattered about K-Aisle. One skid... not been touched since 4-30-2024."

Example: "Lack of space due to overstock: lack of clearance to move certain items to commerce (example - 39 skids of one type of socket)."

Example: "Prestage area fills up and spills into work space."

Workflow & Personnel

Last-minute requests and inefficient processes are stretching personnel thin, impacting morale and task completion.

Impact: "Last-minute requests for material from Commerce: Typically, common."

Impact: "Existing personnel being stretched too thin and having to consistently compensate at a moment's notice."

Impact: "Poor moral and and tasks not being completed in a timely manner."
